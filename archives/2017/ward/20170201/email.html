Alameda Ward Newsletter January 2017
February 1, 2017

Dear member of the Alameda Ward family,

          We hope you enjoy the January issue of the Alameda Ward Newsletter. We draw your attention to the front-page article by Bishop Besich, who directed the same message to priesthood holders at the Saturday Priesthood Leadership meeting of Stake Conference. The accompanying photo is a memorable illustration of his message.

          The Relief Society’s midweek activity was also timely, with a presentation by Britton Larson on “Finance & Investing Basics.” Note the green refreshments and tree leaves on page 4. Thanks to Becca Jo for the photos.

          We present an excerpt from Carma Heywood’s talk about raindrops on January 1st. Certainly there was more to the talk than a discourse on raindrops. We made the print large enough for children to read. Wonderful message about service to others.

          The cub scouts filled the cultural hall with lots of noise during pack meeting. Derreck Smith added to the confusion with his tom-tom, and Molly Letcher tossed the boys around in the tent. (No one was hurt.)

          Sister Lott just arrived from Brigham City and the MTC, and launched her full-time mission in the Alameda and Peterson Park wards. She sparkles and she sings. We are delighted to have her among us as companion to Sister Jun, hopefully for a long, long time. Read her bio sketch and see her photo on page 7.

          Don’t forget to print and attempt the crossword puzzle on page 11. It’s a good one that draws upon “The Proclamation to the World” for answers.

          Save the newsletter to your hard drive for safekeeping. Once downloaded, the best way to view the document is to press “View” at the top of the page, hover over “Page Display” and click on “Two-Up” and “Show Cover Page During Two-Up.” It should then read (and print) like a book.

          Finally, we miss Eric Thornhill. He has been such a wonderful counselor to Bishop Besich and bishopric companion to Dwight Davis. We wish him well. We heartily welcome Moriah Hepworth to the bishopric and look forward to a long association with him.
Sincerely, 
Bishop Baldo Besich
1st Counselor Dwight Davis
2nd Counselor Moriah Hepworth
