ALAMEDA ESTATES HOMEOWNERS ASSOCIATION
 
Board Meeting Minutes
September 19, 2019
 
I. Call to Order
Board President Dennis Ederer called the third 2019 quarterly meeting of the Alameda Estates Home Owners Association to order at 7:01 p.m. on September 19, 2019 at the Burns-McDaniel residence.
II. Attendees and Introductions
Board members attending the meeting were Dennis Ederer, Carmen Muscha, Cassie Bowman, Emily McDaniel, Liz Heise, Trevor Boone, and Greg Bell. Teresa Perrine and Jacqueline Haeg were not in attendance. Also attending were neighbors Eduarda Yates and Ben Heise. Stephanie Mueller from Reserve Advisors was in attendance at the beginning to answer questions about the reserve study report.
III. Reserve Study Report
Stephanie with Reserve Advisors had walked the perimeter with Dennis and provided a reserve study report. She has completed over 700 reserve studies around the country. Our main issues are with the irrigation system and common walls. Reserve Advisors provided a 30-year projection study with estimated costs and cash flow analysis. They estimate $40,000 to replace the irrigation system, based on square-footage basis (55 cents/sf – though it could be higher than that). For the walls, it would cost around ~$22,000 to renovate. We could do it as-needed, but if certain issues aren’t addressed/maintained, it could lead to bigger issues down the line.
IV. Approval of minutes from last meeting
A motion was made and seconded to approve the minutes of the June 12, 2019 meeting. The minutes were approved unanimously as written.
V. Treasurer’s Report
The water bill went up a little bit because of sprinkler breakage. We had normal maintenance payments and paid the insurance. We had professional fees for the reserve study. HOA receipts of dues: We’ve heard from all but 7 households. Carmen will send out statements to remaining households. Approval of the Treasurer’s report was motioned and seconded and was approved unanimously.
VI. Committee Reports
1. Welcoming:
No one new bought a house this past quarter, but the Lee family moved in to 2524 S Forest and Trevor welcomed them.
2. Landscaping:
Dennis and Greg have re-started walking the perimeter to keep an eye out for issues. There are numerous problems with the irrigation system on the perimeter. Many drip lines are clogged and there are new areas with pooling. We have had Greg Schneider out to fix things and Dennis himself has been fixing broken sprinkler heads. We had an issue with a zone along Mill that came on and was not shutting off. Greg Schneider came to fix it. We are dealing with a continuation of acute problems of breaks and the long-term problems of the aging irrigation system. We also have an issue with the locks on the irrigation boxes oxidizing so that they keys don’t work. We will not be trimming the palms this year but will be trimming them next year. The money not used for palms this year will be put toward trimming the other trees and repairing the irrigation. Greg and Dennis have been working with Juan on keeping the trees trimmed. He may start to make piles of cuttings from the trees for neighbors who have fire places and would like it.
Reminder from previous meetings: Almost all our trees have number tags on them. Please reference the tree number if you contact the landscaping committee about a tree issue (the tree numbers are on the side of the tree closest to the wall and are on a quarter-size metal disc). Also, we continue to have frequent problems with irrigation. When the breaks occur, it is hard to understand the entire system because it takes at least 2 people in communication by phone or text, with one to turn things on/off and the other to be by the sprinkler to see what happens.
